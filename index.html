Introduction

My girlfriend and I will move to Barcelona in September 2025. To put my new skills in Python, SQL, and Tableau into practice, I chose a project that’s both personally relevant and a good showcase of what I’ve learned.

To prepare for our move, I analyzed the rental market using data from Idealista, the city’s most popular housing platform. With a student budget in mind, I focused on furnished flats under €2,000 a month to see what’s realistic and where to begin our search. The goal was to narrow down neighbourhood options, get a feel for the city, and find out which areas best suit our needs.

Questions I aimed to answer:

  • Which districts offer the best value for money (€/m²)?
  • Which districts have the largest rental supply?
  • What are the average sizes and rents by district?

Deeper insights I explored:

  • Two-bedroom vs. one-bedroom: How much more do two-bedroom flats cost? We'd like space for friends to stay over.
  • Quiet vs. central: Which districts just outside the centre (like Gràcia, Poble-Sec, Poblenou, Sants, and Sant Antoni) offer a good balance of price and space?

Tools I used:

  • Open Web Scraper – for collecting rental data
  • Python – for cleaning and preparing the data
  • Open Geo Code – for mapping coordinates
  • SQL – for queries and aggregations
  • Tableau – for creating clear visual insights
